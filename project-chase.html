Chase Bank Mobile Feature

Add new features to the Chase Mobile Banking

Overview

The Problem

The current adoption process via Happy Paw’s website is unclear. This research addresses the current pet adoption process. It offers users a better image of the pet situation in Ukraine and a more transparent process of helping and adopting pets in need.

  • My Role

    UX/UI Designer

  • Tools

    Pen/Pencil & Paper, Figma

  • Client

    This work is a personal project, and was not done for, or in affiliation with, J.P. Morgan Chase.

Project Goal

The goal of this project was to design a new personal financial management feature that embeds smoothly within the current Chase app. This entailed creating new services that respected the existing brand and design of Chase Bank.

Research

Research Goals

  • Research banking apps to understand their offerings
  • Research financial management challenges of people
  • Understand the financial decisions users make when selecting a bank or budgeting tool

I started off the project by going through Chase mobile app and learning its current personal finance management offerings.

So I started with a features that were already in the app:
Autosave prompts users to set goals and work toward them by automatically transferring money into their savings account per set time period.
Credit Journey tracks user's credit scores over time, alerts them to changes, and allows them to see how certain actions negatively or positively impact their score.
Investment by J.P. Morgan is a separate account customers can open to invest in stocks online through Chase.




Competitive Analysis

After doing some preliminary research online, I compared the strengths and weaknesses of some of Chase’s competitors and several other personal finance management apps.

User Interview & Survey

I conducted 10 user interviews between the ages of 23 and 35 in order to learn more about their financial management habits and bank app usages and gain a more comprehensive understanding of the objectives, drivers, and challenges faced by millennials when it comes to personal finance.

Findings & Insights

  • People usually just memorize and used their own brainpower to keep track of finances in their own way - dont set aside a specific amount of time to manage finances
  • 6/10 have some trouble sticking to their budget
“Even though I keep track of my spending, it's challenging to set a monthly budget because things constantly change.”
  • 3/10 participants don’t really open the bank app a lot, just check their finances through the messages they receive on spending through their card, usually check their balance etc. on the go
  • 7/10 users wanted to improve their financial planning
  • Participants did have specific long & short-term goals for their savings
  • 9/10 participants knew their credit score and think it’s important to have a good score

My research showed me 2 distinct types of users:

Ultimately both of these users want to set financial goals for themselves and save money in order to reach those goals in the future.

Define

User Persona

Due to the limited time available for this project I chose to concentrate on building this feature for the more typical, everyday user. In order to synthesize the results of my user interviews, I constructed the user persona Spencer Williams. It was clear that the user had vague financial goals but needs help sticking to his budgets and setting money aside for his goals.

User & Business Goals

To better visualize how this feature could benefit both the user and Chase, I listed out my user goals against Chase’s goals. Another key point that I was mindful of was Chase’s goal of keeping user’s within the Chase ecosystem, especially since there are so many competitors already out there.

Ideate

Determining the most relevant features for the user

Before I started designing out the budgeting feature, I wanted to take another step back to make sure I wasn’t overlooking anything else important that should belong to a personal financial management feature.

Task Flow

In order for these new budgeting features to be useful, I knew I needed to increase the visibility and work with the existing information architecture of the app.

Design

I began my wireframes by mapping the current Chase screens and UI elements to build out the new budgeting section. I built mid- fidelity wireframes, as the UI already existed. I began to sketch out every screen needed for the user to accomplish the above flows within the budgeting section.I tried to integrate the screens seamlessly into the the existing app.

During the process of designing features, I ran into problems in deciding which style the "Creating a saving goal" page should adapt: a one-page version that was more similar to the current Chase Mobile design, or a multi-page version that was more common in recent app designs. Therefore, I conducted an A/B testing for this feature.




Testing result showed the multi-page version was easier to navigate and had higher completion rate. Therefore I decided to adapt the multi-page version.


Prototype & Test

Usability testing was conducted with hi-fi prototype made from InVision. I conducted remote testings with people from my personal network.
I asked 6 participants to create a goal with a monthly autopay fixed amount. All participants had no problem with the task. Still, all of them were interested in where this feature would be in the app, so the prototype should begin with the main Chase app screen test the user flow and if this feature will be noticeable within the existing app.

Final Thoughts & Next Steps

The feedback from the usability testing showed that there was a lot of interest in having a budget feature rolled into one’s own mobile banking app, since all of the users interviewed and surveyed indicated they are utilizing this to keep a tab on their finances.

If I had more time, I would have tested more ideas, conducted further testing, and found out how to trigger more interest in users for saving money using budget features within the banking app. The most critical point would be to evaluate how well users understand the budgeting concepts. If this feature were to be put into production, I would monitor how many users continuously use this app over time and for how long they use it before dropping it off. I would also keep track of metrics on the goals that users set for themselves and their rate of accomplishment. Furthermore, through this personal finance management feature, I would monitor the number of users who sign up for additional Chase services. With each stage along the user journey, monitoring metrics provides the opportunity to adjust the app to serve both the users and the business better.

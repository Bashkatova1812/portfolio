Happy Paw

Pet Adoption Website Re-design

Overview

The Problem

The current adoption process via Happy Paw’s website is unclear. This research addresses the current pet adoption process. It offers users a better image of the pet situation in Ukraine and a more transparent process of helping and adopting pets in need.

Research

Research Goals

  • Learn current user frustrations and pain points
  • Learn how a user typically searches for pets online
  • Learn more about the current animal shelter adoption process in Ukraine
  • Discover what features users would need to navigate through the website successfully
  • Understand the needs, wants, and must-haves for users when searching for a new pet online

Secondary Research Findings

  • The no-breed and village dogs have a higher chance of being adopted abroad, mainly in the US, UK, and Europe, as the culture of Ukraine often neglects them
  • People in Ukraine prefer to buy pure breeds due to misconceptions about local dogs and sometimes use them as a symbol of status
  • The number of homeless animals has increased in Ukraine due to the war and evacuation

Competitive Analysis

I created a Competitive Analysis to understand what other similar organizations offer on their websites. I learned that many animal shelter websites in Ukraine are outdated and unresponsive.

So I extended my research to the animal shelter and adoption organizations in Europe and US. I learned that most of them have the same approach regarding the landing page. They offer filter search, and the photos of animals for adoption are of good quality, and also they offer a lot of information on animals.

Survey

I surveyed 7 participants who have adopted or want to adopt a pet or help animals in Ukraine.

Survey Findings:

  • 75% of participants mentioned knowing the breed is critical, as well as age
  • 62% of participants often said information about the pet’s health and behavior is missing
  • 34% are not ready to adopt but want to know how they can help animals in Ukraine
  • 86% of participants find their pets online

User Interview

I conducted 5 user interviews to learn about their experience with animal organizations, their reasons for adopting, and if they are willing to help these organizations in any way.

Key questions included:

  • What information do users look for when deciding whether to adopt?
  • What improvements would users like to make to the current website?
  • What pain points do users encounter during the adoption process?
  • What do users not wish to see on the adoption website?
  • How you can help animals in Ukraine?

The main insights that have resulted from the research included:

  • Users would like to see better photos and videos before filling in the form
“When I see a nice photo of a dog it definitely attracts my attention, if there is a video I can even see dog personality”
  • Call a shelter isn’t the best way to find available pets; users can’t easily view available pets on the current website
“It’s frustrating when the website is outdated and I need to call to find all the information that I want”
  • Users would like to meet (or virtually meet) and connect with the dog before the final decision
  • For users outside of Ukraine, organization transparency and clarity of the adoption process are crucial
  • 100% would use social media if they would need to report missing or found pets and from that 90% would consider contacting the shelters
  • 80% want to help animals from Ukraine but they don’t know how

Wireframes & Visual Design

I drew some initial sketches for the home page and pets page as I found them the most important in figuring out how the site's functions and information should be presented to users.

I aimed for my final designs to be human-centered at every touchpoint.

After creating a mood board and analyzing our competitors and industry disrupters in the pet adoptions, I began designing the UI elements that would serve as the framework for all my designs, namely, Happy Paw’s logo and Style Guide.

My final logo design utilized a clean, modern font paired with a geometric brandmark in Ukrainian colors personifying Happy Paw brand as a caring and loving.

The color palette needed to be modern, and trustworthy while not distracting from the animal's pictures. I created a baseline palette to work with, knowing that this style guide would be iterated as I went through the design process.

Prototype & Test

Usbility Testing

I created a mid-fidelity prototype and conducted testing. A usability test was conducted, including two tasks:

  • Finding a specific dog and applying for adoption
  • Finding out whether adoption from Germany was possible

The results were compiled into an affinity map, and the following priority revisions were identified and edited:

  • I have shortened the text on the homepage to reduce information overload
  • I made the adoption process from abroad more explicit. For example, I added a section about abroad adoption in the "The adoption process"
  • Added a call-to-action above the fold
  • The "Our Happy Stories" section was edited
  • Shortened space on the dog filtering page above the fold so that it's clear that the photos change when someone clicks on a filter.
  • Made the FAQ section clearer so that contacting Happy Paw is the last resort.

User Testing

I conducted five-person usability tests to see how users navigate and interact with Happy Paw's website. The main tasks for the usability test were searching for the pet, completing the online adoption process, and finding how users can help animals in need in Ukraine.

Key Findings and Iteration:

  • The test was successful, meaning that every participant fulfilled my assigned tasks with minor trouble.
  • All of the participants found the website to be clean, intuitive, and easy to navigate.
  • 30% of participants found themselves confused on the "Guardianship" section
  • 100% of participants found a needed dog using the filter
  • All of the participants found viewing the available pets easy.

Project Learnings

The most challenging part of this project was how to make the user's adoption process as easy and quick as possible so that users would not feel overwhelmed about it and leave in the middle of the process and show users how they can help animals from Ukraine. In addition, by empathizing with our interviewees, I understood more about why people are not sure how they can be helpful being abroad.

Creating this website encouraged me to think more about the accessible way to save animals from the counties in need.

I still need to work on the "Guardianship" section and test if we need to show the user what pet is under guardianship on the adoption page.
